## 2. Tier-1 platform deep dives

### 2.1 Buffer

- **Positioning**: The simple, reliable scheduler. "Plan, track, and publish content
  in just a few clicks."
- **Pricing** (verified 2026): **Free** (3 channels, 10 queued posts/channel, basic
  analytics). **Essentials** $6/mo per channel (annual). **Team** $12/mo per channel
  (annual), adds unlimited team members and approval workflows. Channels 11–25 drop to
  ~$3.33/mo each on annual.
- **Channels**: Instagram, Facebook, X, LinkedIn, Pinterest, TikTok, Mastodon, YouTube
  (Shorts), Threads, Bluesky, Google Business Profile.
- **Core features**: Clean composer, queue-based scheduling, "Start Page" link-in-bio,
  Pablo image editor (light), AI Assistant (caption + idea generation), basic
  analytics, hashtag manager, Instagram grid preview (via Later-like planning), RSS
  auto-post.
- **Differentiators**: Best-in-class simplicity; per-channel (not per-seat) pricing on
  Essentials; generous free tier; fast, opinionated UX.
- **Weaknesses**: Light inbox (no unified messaging); analytics are basic vs Sprout;
  no native approvals on Essentials; no RTL/Jalali; no Rubika/Telegram-native.
- **UX pattern to learn from**: The **queue metaphor** — content goes into a time-slot
  queue per channel, and the calendar is a visualization of that queue. Simple, fast,
  predictable.

### 2.2 Hootsuite

- **Positioning**: The incumbent all-in-one for agencies and enterprises.
- **Pricing** (verified 2026): **Professional** $99/mo (1 user, 10 social accounts,
  unlimited scheduling). **Team** $249/mo (3 users, 20 accounts, team features).
  Enterprise custom.
- **Channels**: IG, FB, X, LinkedIn, TikTok, Pinterest, YouTube, Threads.
- **Core features**: **Streams** (the signature multi-column live monitor for mentions,
  messages, scheduled), composer with bulk scheduling (CSV), OW.ly link shortener +
  analytics, post-approval workflows, social listening (basic), robust analytics &
  exportable reports, team roles, Hootsuite Inbox (unified messaging).
- **Differentiators**: Streams paradigm; breadth of integrations; brand-certified
  training; strong enterprise governance.
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive entry point; UX feels dated and heavy; RTL/Persian locale
  exists but is buggy; slow to innovate on AI; per-seat pricing punishes teams.
- **UX pattern to learn from**: **Streams** — a live, column-based monitoring surface.
  Nashrino's Inbox should borrow the "multi-stream by channel/topic" idea but in RTL.

### 2.3 Sprout Social

- **Positioning**: The premium, analytics- and inbox-first platform for serious brands.
- **Pricing** (approx., verified tier names): **Standard** ~$249/user/mo,
  **Professional** ~$399/user/mo, **Advanced** ~$499/user/mo. Per-seat.
- **Channels**: IG, FB, X, LinkedIn, TikTok, Pinterest, YouTube, WhatsApp, Google
  Business, Reddit.
- **Core features**: **Smart Inbox** (the gold-standard unified inbox with
  sentiment-aware tagging, routing, SLA tracking), publishing & scheduling, **Listening**

  approvals, CRM (Sprout Social Customers), employee advocacy (Bambu), premium support.
- **Differentiators**: Best-in-class inbox + reporting depth; task routing & SLA;
  benchmarking against competitors; enterprise governance.
- **Weaknesses**: Very expensive; overkill for SMBs; no RTL/Jalali; no Rubika/Telegram.
- **UX pattern to learn from**: **Smart Inbox** — every inbound message (comment, DM,
  mention, review) lands in one filterable, assignable, SLA-tracked stream. Nashrino's
  Inbox must approach this bar. Also: **failed-post notifications** open the post
  directly in Compose for one-tap resend — a small but excellent pattern.

## 3. Tier-2 feature-angle notes

- Premium unified inbox with **automated inbox assistant** (saved replies, routing
  rules, auto-tagging), social listening, ROI tracking (UTM), publishing, robust
  analytics. ~$49–$79/user/mo entry.
- **Takeaway for Nashrino**: The inbox must support saved replies (snippets), routing

  is the model.

### 3.2 Brandwatch — the listening angle

- Enterprise social listening (mention tracking, sentiment, share-of-voice, crisis
  detection). Now part of Cision. Premium pricing.
- **Takeaway**: Listening is a **Phase 4+** feature for Nashrino (mentions + keyword
  tracking on IG/Telegram), not launch. Build the data plumbing now (inbound event
  store) so listening can be layered on.

### 3.3 Canva / CapCut — the content-creation angle

- Canva (templates, brand kit, AI image) and CapCut (video editing, captions) are the
  de-facto content-creation stack. Mature social tools increasingly embed Canva.
- **Takeaway**: Nashrino's media editor should be **Persian-first** (Vazirmatn text,
  RTL templates, Jalali date stamps on graphics) rather than trying to replicate Canva's
  breadth. Offer a Canva-like overlay-text + template + filter editor optimized for
  Persian typography and the exact aspect ratios IG/Rubika/Telegram demand.

### 3.4 ManyChat — the Instagram DM-automation angle

- The market leader for Instagram comment-to-DM and DM-funnel automation. Flow-builder,
  triggers, conditions, 24h-window handling. ~$15–$300+/mo based on contacts.
- **Takeaway**: Nashrino's automation engine should offer a **simpler, rule-based**
  alternative to ManyChat's flow builder for the 80% case (comment keyword → send DM
  with content), tuned for Persian commerce ("comment 'کد' to get the catalog"),
  compliant with Meta's 24h window, with Persian digit normalization.

## 4. Cross-cutting best practices

### 4.1 Publishing queue + retry/worker architecture (the production bar)

Mature tools (Sprout, Hootsuite, Buffer) all converge on the same architecture, which
Nashrino must adopt:

- **Separate task queue per rate-limited downstream API.** Instagram Graph API,
  Rubika Bot API, Telegram Bot API, and LinkedIn API each have distinct rate limits and
  failure modes. A single shared queue creates head-of-line blocking. → Nashrino: one
  Celery queue per channel adapter.
- **Idempotency keys.** Every publish job carries a unique idempotency key so a retry
  never double-publishes. Downstream APIs (Meta, Rubika) honor idempotency where
  supported; where not, the adapter checks "does this message already exist?" before
  retrying.
- **Exponential backoff with jitter.** Retry on transient failures with backoff factor
  ~2, capped (e.g., 1s, 2s, 4s, 8s, 16s, max 5 min), plus ±20% jitter to avoid
  thundering herds.
- **Retry budget / circuit breaker.** After N consecutive failures against a channel
  (e.g., 5), trip a circuit breaker: stop dispatching new jobs to that channel, mark
  the platform connection as `error`, surface it in the Action Center, and run a health
  check before resuming.
- **Dead-letter queue (DLQ).** Jobs that exhaust retries go to a DLQ for manual
  inspection and one-click "retry" or "discard" from the UI.
- **Durable state machine.** Each publish job has an explicit state (`pending →
processing → live → success | failed | action`) persisted before the API call, so a
  worker crash mid-publish resumes correctly.
- **Publish-attempt audit log.** Every attempt records request payload, response
  payload, error, started_at, finished_at — the `publish_attempts` table in Nashrino's
  schema already models this correctly.
- **Scheduled-time accuracy.** A beat scheduler (Celery beat) dispatches due jobs; the
  target is to attempt publish within 30s of the scheduled time, P95 within 2 min.

### 4.2 Instagram Graph API publishing (the technical contract)

Verified against Meta for Developers docs (2025–2026):

- **Requirement**: Instagram **professional account** (Business or Creator) linked to a
  Facebook Page; app in Meta App Review with `instagram_content_publish` and related
  permissions.
- **Two-step flow**:
  1. `POST /{ig-user-id}/media` — create a **media container** (image_url /
     video_url / carousel children). Returns a container `id`.
  2. `POST /{ig-user-id}/media_publish` — publish the container. Returns the published
     media `id`.
- **Carousel**: create a child container per item, then a parent container referencing
  children (`media_type=CAROUSEL`, `children=id1,id2,...`), then publish the parent.
  Counts as **one** published post against the limit.
- **Reels**: `media_type=REELS`, `video_url`. Large videos use **resumable upload** via
  `rupload.facebook.com` (the Instagram-branded resumable endpoint) before container
  creation. `share_to_feed=true` recommended.
- **Stories**: `media_type=STORY` with story-specific parameters.
- **Rate limit**: **100 API-published posts per 24-hour rolling window per account**
  (carousels count as one). Enforced on the `/media_publish` call. A
  `/content_publishing_limit` endpoint returns current usage.
- **Container expiry**: containers expire (~24h) if not published. Nashrino must publish
  promptly after container creation, or re-create.
- **AI disclosure**: `is_ai_generated=true` must be set on AI-generated media (Meta
  policy, 2025+).
- **Personal accounts**: **no Content Publish API**. Nashrino's personal-account mode =
  reminder (notification to publish manually) + pre-filled caption/media to copy.

### 4.3 Instagram Messaging API / comment-to-DM compliance (the policy contract)

- **24-hour standard window**: After a user sends a message (or comments, if the
  comment triggers a DM), the app may send automated DMs for **24 hours**. Outside the
  window, automated promotional DMs are prohibited.
- **Message Tags**: `HUMAN_AGENT` tag extends the window to **7 days** for legitimate
  customer support follow-up (requires the tag, content must be a response to a user
  question). `CONFIRMED_EVENT_UPDATE`, `ACCOUNT_UPDATE`, etc. exist but are restricted.
- **Meta deprecation note**: Message Tags are being deprecated on **Messenger** (Feb 9, 2026) but **remain available on Instagram Messaging** as of writing. Nashrino must
  monitor this and not over-rely on tags.
- **One-Time Notification, Sponsored Messaging, News Messaging**: not available on
  Instagram Messaging.
- **Mandatory bot disclosure**: Bots must identify as automated where required by local
  regulation and Meta policy.
- **Comment-to-DM flow**: The app subscribes to webhook `comments` events; when a
  comment matches a trigger rule, it sends a DM via the Messaging API (`POST
/{ig-user-id}/messages`). The first DM must be sent within the 24h window opened by
  the comment.
- **Nashrino policy**: Default to 24h-window-only automation for promotions. Use
  `HUMAN_AGENT` only for genuine support replies. Never send unsolicited DMs. Log every
  automation event for audit.

### 4.4 Approval & collaboration workflows (the UX bar)

From Planable, Sprout, Loomly:

- **Multi-level approvals**: draft → internal review → client review → approved →
  scheduled. Each transition is a named state with an owner.
- **In-context comments**: reviewers comment on the post mock-up (Planable's Figma-like
  pattern), not in a separate thread.
- **Lock/unlock**: once approved, the post is locked from edits unless explicitly
  unlocked.
- **Roles**: Admin (everything), Editor (create/edit, cannot publish), Approver
  (review/approve, may not create), Viewer (read-only).
- **Notifications**: approvers are notified on submission; creators notified on
  approval/rejection with the reviewer's comments.
- **Nashrino baseline (Phase 2)**: single-level approval (submit → approve/reject →
  schedule) with comments. Multi-level (Planable-style) in Phase 3 for agencies.

### 4.5 Analytics metrics taxonomy (the reporting bar)

The metrics mature tools standardize on (Nashrino will adopt this taxonomy):

**Reach & impressions**

- Reach (unique accounts that saw the content)
- Impressions (total views, including repeats)
- Frequency (impressions / reach)

**Engagement**

- Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares + saves) / reach (or / impressions)
- Total interactions, by type (like, comment, share, save, click)
- Avg. interactions per post

**Audience**

- Follower count & net growth (gains − losses)
- Audience demographics (where available: age, gender, geo, active hours)
- Best time to post (per channel, derived from follower active-hours + historical
  engagement)

**Conversion**

- Link clicks (UTM-tagged)
- Conversion rate (clicks / reach) — requires UTM + analytics integration
- Revenue attribution (Phase 4+; requires e-commerce integration)

**Operational**

- Publishing volume (posts per channel per period)
- Publish success rate
- Scheduled-vs-actual publish time variance
- Inbox first-response time, resolution time
- Comment-to-DM fulfillment rate

**Comparative (Phase 4+)**

- Share of voice (mentions vs competitors)
- Sentiment (positive/neutral/negative)
- Competitor benchmarking

### 4.6 Calendar & queue UX (the planning bar)

From Later, Loomly, Buffer:

- **Visual month grid** with color-coded posts per channel; click a day to see all posts.
- **Drag-and-drop reschedule**: grab a post, drop on another time slot; auto-confirm or
  confirm-on-drop.
- **Best-time suggestions**: AI proposes optimal slots per channel based on audience
  active hours + historical engagement.
- **Per-channel lanes / swimlanes**: toggle to view one channel's calendar at a time, or
  overlay all.
- **Readiness indicators**: visual state on each post (draft, ready, scheduled,
  published, failed) so the team sees what needs attention.
- **Week & day views** for fine-grained planning; **agenda/list view** for accessibility.
- **Nashrino-specific**: Jalali month names, Persian weekday names (شنبه...جمعه), Iranian
  holidays overlaid, Sat–Wed work-week shading, Persian-digit dates throughout.

### 4.7 AI features now standard (2024–2026 state of the art)

- **Caption generation**: from a topic/brief, generate 1–N caption variants in the
  brand voice. (Buffer, Metricool, ContentStudio, SocialPilot all have this.)
- **Hashtag suggestions**: based on topic + trending relevance.
- **Best-time-to-post**: per-channel slot recommendation.
- **Image variation / on-image text**: Canva-style AI text overlays.
- **Content repurposing**: turn a long post into a thread, or a blog into a caption.
- **Idea generation**: "what should I post this week?" based on trends + history.
- **Sentiment / tone check**: flag captions that read as off-brand.
- **Nashrino AI priority**: **Persian-first caption generation** tuned to brand voice
  (set in the brand kit), Persian hashtag suggestions, Persian comment-to-DM keyword
  expansion. This is a defensible gap — generic English AI produces poor Persian.

### 4.8 RTL & localization (the gap)

- **No Tier-1 platform ships a correct RTL layout.** Hootsuite has a Persian locale but
  the layout breaks (mirrored icons, LTR punctuation bleed, broken bidirectional text in
  the composer). Buffer, Sprout, Later, Publer: no RTL at all.
- **No Tier-1 platform supports the Jalali calendar.** All assume Gregorian.
- **No Tier-1 platform supports Persian digit rendering** in dates/numbers.
- **Bidirectional text** (mixing Persian with English hashtags, URLs, @handles) is
  notoriously hard; tools that attempt it produce cursor-jump and selection bugs.
- **Nashrino's RTL must be first-class**: built on `dir="rtl"` at the root, Vazirmatn
  font, a bidirectional text component for the composer, Persian-digit formatting

## 6. Strategic gaps Nashrino will exploit

Synthesizing the benchmark, Nashrino's defensible position rests on **five gaps** no
incumbent closes:

### Gap 1 — RTL-native, Persian-first UX (zero competitors)

No Tier-1 platform ships a correct RTL layout or Persian-first experience. Nashrino is
built RTL from the root (`<html dir="rtl" lang="fa">`), Vazirmatn-native, with a
bidirectional composer. This alone makes Nashrino usable for a Persian team in a way
no incumbent is.

### Gap 2 — Jalali calendar (zero competitors)

Every competitor assumes Gregorian. Nashrino's calendar renders Jalali months,
Persian weekdays, Iranian holidays, and the Saturday–Wednesday work week. Planning
around Nowruz, Yalda, and Ramadan becomes first-class.

### Gap 3 — Rubika + Eitaa native integration (zero competitors)

Rubika (~40M users) and Eitaa (~30M) are dominant Iranian platforms with Bot APIs and
**zero** third-party management tool support. Nashrino's Rubika adapter (Bot API v3)
and Eitaa adapter (Phase 3) are a genuine category first. Combined with Instagram and
Telegram, Nashrino covers the **actual** Iranian social stack.

### Gap 4 — Persian-tuned AI + comment-to-DM for local commerce

Generic English AI produces stilted Persian and poor hashtag relevance. ManyChat
exists for comment-to-DM but is English-first, foreign, and fragile under Iranian
network conditions. Nashrino's Persian-tuned AI caption assistant and compliant
comment-to-DM engine (with Persian digit normalization, keyword matching tuned for
commerce patterns like "کد", "قیمت", "لینک") serve the Persian seller directly.

### Gap 5 — Price & payment reality (IRR, local gateways)

Hootsuite ($99/user/mo) and Sprout ($249/user/mo) are priced for Western enterprise
budgets and billed in USD — inaccessible to Iranian SMBs. Nashrino prices in IRR,
bills via local gateways (Zarinpal, etc.), and targets 10–20× cheaper for the same
capability set, making professional social ops affordable for the segment that needs
it most.

### The combined moat

Any single gap could be closed by a determined incumbent. The **combination** —
RTL + Jalali + Rubika/Eitaa + Persian AI + IRR pricing — requires a ground-up
Persian-first rebuild that no Western incumbent will undertake for a market they
cannot easily monetize. This is Nashrino's durable defensible position.
